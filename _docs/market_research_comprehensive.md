# Market Research: Early Years Toolkit Viability Analysis

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Market exists but is already served by established competitors.**

Key findings:
- 18,000 family daycare educators in Australia (potential target market)
- Subscription template services already exist with 60,000+ users
- Competitors have 10+ years market presence
- No evidence of failed competitors (suggests market is viable but saturated)
- EYLF changes infrequently (last major update: 2022 v2.0)
- Strong distribution channels exist but require advertising spend
- High churn risk with download-and-cancel model

**Bottom line:** Market is viable but Clare would be late entrant competing against established players. Success would require significant differentiation beyond "templates + monthly updates."

## 1. MARKET SIZE

### Family Daycare Educators (Primary Target)

**Numbers:**
- **18,000 family daycare educators** nationally (Family Day Care Australia, 2024)
- **688 approved FDC services** (coordination units)
- **~500 FDC schemes** currently operating
- FDC educators care for **125,000+ children**
- **4.9%** of all children in childcare attend family daycare

**Geographic distribution (2020 data):**
- NSW: 161 services (highest)
- Victoria, Queensland: Also top 3

**Key characteristics:**
- Work from own homes
- Care for 4-7 children (max 4 under school age)
- Operating solo or in small networks
- Lower income than center-based educators
- Consistently receive lower NQS ratings than centers

### Small Preschools/Community Centers

**Numbers:**
- **18,990 total ECEC services** in Australia (2024)
- Breakdown:
  - 61.8% Centre Based Day Care
  - 34.5% Outside School Hours Care
  - ~4% Family Day Care schemes

**Calculation:**
- Total services: 18,990
- Minus large operators (likely on Xplor/Storypark): ~11,850
- Minus family daycare schemes: ~500
- **Potential small/independent operators: ~6,500-7,000**

### Total Addressable Market

**Conservative estimate:**
- 18,000 family daycare educators
- 3,000-5,000 small center educators (whose centers won't pay for platforms)
- Student teachers/trainees (unknown number, but rotating cohort)

**Total potential: 21,000-23,000 individual educators**

**Realistic capture:** 1,000-2,000 subscribers (5-10% market penetration)

## 4. WILLINGNESS TO PAY

### What we know:

**One-off purchases (Etsy/TPT):**
- Educators currently pay $5-25 for one-off templates
- No ongoing commitment
- Risk: obsolete when regulations change

**Subscription competitors:**
- Aussie Childcare Network: pricing hidden
- Starskills: pricing hidden

**Why pricing is hidden:**
- Likely high enough that transparency would deter signups
- OR varied pricing by tier/features
- OR pricing optimization testing

**Inference:** If competitors with 60,000+ users hide pricing, either:
1. Price is premium ($30-50/month), OR
2. They're losing money and can't raise prices without churn

### Comparable subscription services:

**Professional learning:**
- Early Childhood Australia Learning Hub: $-subscription (courses)
- Butler Diaries digital training: ~$40-80 one-off

**Consumer subscription benchmarks:**
- Netflix: $10.99-22.99/month
- Canva Pro: $17.99/month
- Teachers Pay Teachers premium: Free with ads, or pay per resource

**Realistic pricing for Clare:**
- Too low ($5-10/month): Canva/Etsy cheaper, can't sustain business
- Sweet spot ($15-25/month): Positioned between one-off and premium
- Too high ($30+/month): Competing with full platforms

**Risk:** Educators might subscribe for 1 month ($20), download everything, cancel.

## 7. CHURN RISK

### The download-and-cancel problem:

**Scenario:**
1. Educator subscribes for $20/month
2. Downloads all templates in month 1
3. Cancels before month 2
4. Uses downloaded templates forever

**How to prevent:**

**Option A: DRM on templates** (bad for user experience)
- Templates expire after subscription ends
- Requires online validation
- Educators hate this

**Option B: Ongoing value** (good but hard)
- Monthly coaching calls
- Live Q&A sessions
- Template updates (but EYLF changes rarely)
- Community forum (but Aussie Childcare Network has this)

**Option C: Annual pricing only** (reduces churn but harder to sell)
- $180/year upfront (vs $20/month)
- Reduces impulse cancellations
- But higher barrier to entry

**Benchmark churn rates:**

| Service Type | Typical Monthly Churn |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Netflix | 2-3% |
| SaaS B2B | 5-7% |
| Consumer subscription boxes | 10-15% |
| Template/resource libraries | **Unknown but likely high** |

**Why template libraries have high churn:**
- Once you have templates, what keeps you subscribed?
- Unlike Netflix (new content weekly), templates are one-time need
- Unlike SaaS (ongoing tool use), templates are static downloads

**Estimated churn for Clare's model:**
- Month 1-3: 30-40% (download and leave)
- Month 4-12: 10-15% (gradual attrition)
- Year 2+: 20-30% (if no new compelling content)

**To sustain 1,000 subscribers:**
- Need 100-150 new signups per month just to replace churn
- At $20/month, that's $20,000/month revenue
- But if CAC is $50-150, need $5,000-22,500/month for acquisition
- **Net margin: poor to break-even**

## 9. EYLF UPDATE FREQUENCY

### Historical pattern:

**EYLF:**
- Released: 2009
- Updated: 2022 (v2.0), 13 years later
- Next update: Unknown (likely 2030+)

**NQS:**
- Introduced: 2012
- Revised: 2018, 2023, 2026
- Changes: Usually minor adjustments, not overhauls

**Other regulations:**
- Child Safe Standards: Ongoing updates
- Ratios/staffing: Occasional changes
- CCS requirements: Government budget-dependent

**Reality:** Major framework changes happen once per decade.

**Implication for "always-updated templates":**
- Strong selling point in theory
- Weak practical value (changes are rare)
- Etsy templates only become obsolete every 10+ years
