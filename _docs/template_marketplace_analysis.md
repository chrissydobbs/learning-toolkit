# The Template Marketplace: Where Non-Platform Educators Are

## What's actually happening

There's a **massive market** of educators downloading EYLF templates from:
- **Etsy** (individual sellers, Canva templates, PDFs)
- **Teachers Pay Teachers** (TPT - teacher resource marketplace)
- **Pinterest** (free downloads, often lower quality)
- **Random websites** (early childhood blogs, free resources)

**Pricing on Etsy/TPT:**
- $5-25 per template pack
- One-time purchase
- Instant digital download

**What they're buying:**
- EYLF outcome posters
- Weekly/monthly planning templates (Word, PDF, Canva)
- Learning story templates
- Observation recording sheets
- Reflection prompts
- Programming cycle visuals
- Links to theorists and outcomes

**Example products:**
- "EYLF Programming Folder/Planner" - Canva template
- "Learning Story Templates with EYLF links" - editable PDF
- "Weekly Programming Template" - Word document
- "EYLF v2.0 Outcome Posters" - printable PDFs
- "Family Day Care Planning Template" - editable Canva

---

## Who's buying these templates?

### 1. Educators whose centers haven't bought a platform
- Center hasn't paid for Storypark/Xplor
- Director hasn't made a decision yet
- Using "whatever works" in the meantime
- Each educator finds their own system

### 2. Family daycare educators (home-based)
- 4-7 kids in their home
- Operating alone, not part of a center
- Can't justify $5,000+/year platform cost
- Need NQS compliance but on a budget

### 3. Student teachers and trainees
- Learning EYLF in college/diploma courses
- Need practice templates for placements
- Not employed yet, buying with own money
- Want to understand frameworks

### 4. Budget-conscious educators
- Low-paid ($25-35/hour)
- Buying tools with their own money
- Center won't provide resources
- Cobbling together their own system

### 5. Small community preschools/kindergartens
- 20-40 children, 2-4 educators
- Limited budget
- No dedicated IT support
- Need simple, affordable tools

---

## What they're currently doing

**The cobbled-together system:**

1. Download planning template from Etsy ($10)
2. Download observation template from TPT ($8)
3. Download EYLF poster from Pinterest (free)
4. Use Word or Google Docs to fill in templates
5. Save photos on phone or computer
6. Print everything out for assessment visits
7. Email or show parents photos on phone
8. Hope it's good enough for NQS assessment

**Problems with this approach:**
- No cohesive system
- Templates don't talk to each other
- No updates when regulations change
- Lots of manual work (printing, organizing, filing)
- No family sharing capability
- Hard to track over time
- Still takes 3-5 hours/week
- Quality inconsistent

---

## What this market actually needs

**Not more one-off templates** (Etsy/TPT already sell these)

**What they need:**
1. **Affordable subscription** ($10-30/month, not $5,000/year)
2. **Complete system** (not random templates to cobble together)
3. **Updates when regulations change** (EYLF v2.0 came out, their $10 Etsy template is now outdated)
4. **Some digital capability** (at least shareable PDFs, not necessarily full platform)
5. **Training/support** (most don't understand WHY they're documenting certain ways)
6. **Assessment-ready** (confidence it will pass NQS visits)

## Where Clare's idea could actually work

**Target market:** The 3,000-5,000 educators currently buying random templates on Etsy/TPT

**What she offers that Etsy/TPT sellers don't:**
1. **Subscription with updates** (not one-off purchases that become outdated)
2. **Complete system** (all templates work together, not random downloads)
3. **Education component** ("understand WHY, not just WHAT")
4. **Monthly refresh** (new ideas, seasonal planning, regulatory updates)
5. **Community** (possibly - other educators using same system)

**What she competes with:**
- Etsy sellers charging $5-25 one-off
- TPT sellers with similar pricing
- Free downloads from blogs/Pinterest

**Her pricing needs to be:**
- More expensive than one-off ($5-25) because it's ongoing
- Much cheaper than platforms ($5,000+/year)
- Comparable to other educator subscriptions ($10-30/month)

**Realistic positioning:**

"Stop downloading random templates from Etsy. Get a complete, always-updated EYLF system for less than a coffee a week."

**Price point:** $15-25/month or $150-250/year

**What's included:**
- Complete template library (planning, observation, reflection)
- Monthly updates (new seasonal ideas, regulatory changes)
- Access to growing resource library
- Training videos on using templates effectively
- Community forum or Facebook group
- PDF downloads (printable, shareable)
- Canva templates (editable)

**What's NOT included:**
- No platform/software to maintain
- No family app
- No cloud storage
- No AI generation
- Just templates + education + community

## The viable business model

**Target:** 1,000 subscribers at $20/month = $240,000/year revenue

**Customer profile:**
- Family daycare educators (biggest segment)
- Small preschool/kindergarten educators
- Budget-conscious center educators whose center won't pay for platform
- Student teachers (cheaper tier: $10/month)

**Value proposition:**
"Professional EYLF documentation system that costs less per month than Butler Diaries, updates automatically unlike Etsy templates, and doesn't require expensive platform fees."

**What makes it work:**
1. Lower price than platforms ($240/year vs $5,000+/year)
2. More comprehensive than one-off templates
3. Ongoing updates (EYLF v3.0 when it comes? Subscribers get it automatically)
4. Education component (pedagogy training built in)
5. Digital + printable (flexible for different work styles)

**What doesn't work:**
- If she's just selling templates (Etsy does that for $10)
- If she's competing on features with Storypark (she'll lose)
- If she's competing on time savings with Mana (AI wins)

---

## The hard questions for Clare

1. **Why would someone pay $240/year for your templates when Etsy sells similar for $10 one-off?**
   
   Answer needs to be: "Because mine update automatically, form a complete system, include training, and provide ongoing professional development. Etsy templates become outdated the moment regulations change."

2. **Why wouldn't they just buy Butler Diaries for $40-80 instead?**
   
   Answer needs to be: "Butler is physical only, can't be shared digitally with families or colleagues, requires reprinting when regulations change, and is room-specific not educator-specific."

3. **Why wouldn't they push their center to just pay for Storypark?**
   
   Answer needs to be: "Many centers can't or won't pay $5,000+/year. Family daycare educators don't have a center to push. This is for educators who need something NOW on their own budget."

4. **Does this actually save time or is it just prettier templates?**
   
   Answer needs to be: "It doesn't save time like AI does. It saves mental effort and decision fatigue by providing a proven system instead of cobbling together random downloads."
